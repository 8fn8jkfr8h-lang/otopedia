Olfactory Neuroblastoma

"Esthesioneuroblastoma"

Rare Malignancy

ICD-O: 9522/3

Faculty Note: ONB is a rare malignant neuroectodermal tumor originating from the olfactory epithelium in the superior nasal vault. It represents < 3% of all intranasal tumors. Key features include a bimodal age distribution, slow progression with capacity for rapid deterioration, and a tendency for late recurrence.

1 Epidemiology & Demographics

Key Characteristics

  • Origin: Olfactory neuroepithelium (superior turbinate, cribriform plate, upper nasal septum).
  • Gender: Slight male predominance or equal distribution.
  • Incidence: 0.4 per million population.
  • Genetic Associations: Rare. Some reports of Trisomy 8, deletion of 11q.

Clinical Triad

Unilateral Obstruction

Epistaxis

Anosmia

The Classic "Bimodal" Age Distribution

Incidence peaks in the 2nd and 6th decades of life.

2 Pathology & Hyams Grading

Histological grading is the single most important prognostic factor aside from staging. The Hyams Grading System (Grades I-IV) evaluates architecture, mitotic index, necrosis, and rosettes.

Grade I: Well Differentiated

  • Architecture: Prominent Lobular preservation
  • Mitoses: None
  • Necrosis: Absent
  • Rosettes: Homer-Wright rosettes prominent
  • Nuclear Pleomorphism: Absent
  • Prognosis: Excellent (56% 5-yr survival)
Immunohistochemistry Profile (Essential for Diff. Diagnosis):
  • Neuron Specific Enolase (NSE) (+)
  • Synaptophysin (+)
  • Chromogranin (+)
  • S-100 (Sustentacular cells) (+)
  • CK, CD99, LCA (Usually Negative)

3 Diagnostic Workup

CT Scan (Bone Window)

Essential for evaluating bony erosion of the cribriform plate, fovea ethmoidalis, and lamina papyracea.

MRI (Gold Standard)

Superior for soft tissue. Look for the "Dumbbell Shape" (waisting at the cribriform plate) and dural enhancement. Differentiates tumor from retained secretions.

Biopsy

Pre-operative biopsy is controversial due to bleeding risk. If done, usually endoscopic. Caution: Highly vascular tumor.

4 Staging Systems

Kadish is the most commonly used system. Dulguerov (TNM) is more precise for extent.

Kadish A

Tumor is confined to the nasal cavity.

Clinical Implication

Highest cure rate. Often amenable to endoscopic resection alone if margins are clear, though combined modality is still common.

Dulguerov (TNM-based) Classification

T1 Tumor involving nasal cavity and/or paranasal sinuses (excluding sphenoid), sparing most superior ethmoidal cells.
T2 Tumor involving nasal cavity and/or paranasal sinuses (including sphenoid) with extension to or erosion of cribriform plate.
T3 Tumor extending into orbit or intracranial cavity (without dural invasion).
T4 Tumor involving brain, dura, orbital contents, or lower cranial nerves.

5 Management Algorithm

The gold standard for curative intent is complete surgical resection with negative margins followed by postoperative radiotherapy.

Resectability Assessment

MRI/CT Evaluation of Skull Base & Orbit

Resectable Disease
1. Surgery:

Craniofacial Resection (Open or Endoscopic)

2. Radiotherapy:

Standard for Kadish B/C. Usually IMRT.

Unresectable / Metastatic (Kadish D)
1. Neoadjuvant Chemotherapy:

Cisplatin/Etoposide based. Aim to shrink for surgery.

2. Definitive Chemoradiation:

Palliative intent.

Trend Watch: Endoscopic Resection

Recent meta-analyses suggest that strictly endoscopic resection (for selected cases not invading the brain parenchyma/extensive orbit) has comparable survival to traditional open craniofacial resection, with significantly lower morbidity.

6 Prognosis & Survival

Survival Insights

Overall 5-year survival ranges from 60% to 75% across all stages. However, stratification by Kadish stage reveals significant disparity.

85% Kadish A (5-yr)
75% Kadish B (5-yr)
45% Kadish C (5-yr)
< 20% High Grade (Hyams 3/4)
Note: Recurrences can occur very late (>10 years). Long-term surveillance is mandatory.
