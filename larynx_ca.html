Laryngeal Carcinoma

"Laryngeal Squamous Cell Carcinoma (LSCC)"

HNSCC Malignancy

ICD-10: C32.0-C32.9

Faculty Note: Laryngeal cancer is the most common head and neck malignancy. Early-stage glottic cancers present with hoarseness and have excellent prognosis (>80% 5-year survival). Supraglottic tumors are clinically silent and present late. TNM staging, tumor site, and HPV status guide multimodal treatment: early stages favor single-modality (surgery or RT), advanced stages require concurrent chemoradiotherapy or laryngectomy.

2 Etiology & Pathogenesis

Primary Risk Factors

Tobacco

Most significant risk factor. Dose-dependent (pack-years).

Alcohol

Synergistic with tobacco (multiplies risk up to 100-fold).

HPV (16/18)

~20-25% of laryngeal cancers. Less prognostically significant than in oropharynx.

Other

LPR, occupational (asbestos, wood dust, paint fumes, mustard gas).

Molecular Alterations

TP53

Loss of tumor suppressor gene (very common in smoking-related SCC)

EGFR

Overexpression of Epidermal Growth Factor Receptor

p16

Inactivation in HPV-negative tumors

Clinical Pearl

Field Cancerization: High-risk for synchronous primaries in lung/esophagus due to widespread mucosal damage from smoke. Panendoscopy recommended.

Risk Multiplier: Alcohol + Tobacco Synergy

Patient with normal ears but severe ear pain must have flexible laryngoscopy to rule out supraglottic/piriform sinus cancer (CN X shared innervation).
